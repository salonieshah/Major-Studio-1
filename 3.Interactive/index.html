Portrayal of Women in European Painting Portraits at MET

How have women been represented, underrepresented, and misrepresented in European Portrait Paintings?

This visualization is inspired from article “Images of women in European Art” from John Berger’s book “Ways of Seeing”. The spectator-owners and painters of most of the European Portraits were males. The artists were usually commissioned by the wealthy ‘Male’ to represent idealized version of his loved one. Therefore a large number of paintings give a skewed perspective of how women were portrayed in art.
